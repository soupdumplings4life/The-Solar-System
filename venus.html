Venus is the second planet from the Sun and Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and mass. But it is the hottest planet in the Solar System with a thick, carbon dioxide-rich atmosphere that creates a runaway greenhouse effect, causing surface temperatures hot enough to melt lead. Named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, Venus is a rocky planet with a surface featuring mountains, volcanoes, and lava plains, although its dense cloud cover of sulfuric acid prevents direct observation of the surface from space. It is unique for its retrograde rotation, spinning in the opposite direction of most planets.
